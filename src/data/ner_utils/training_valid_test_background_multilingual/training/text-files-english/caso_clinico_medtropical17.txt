AMO, 46 years old, male, from the municipality of Anamã (AM).
In October 2007, fever and fever episodes were initiated. ACE was diagnosed through a thick smear for T. cruzi.
The epidemiological history did not allow suspicion of the mode of transmission of CD.
The electrocardiogram showed atrial fibrillation (AF) rhythm with a mean ventricular rate of 110 bpm.
The transthoracic echocardiogram was normal.
Etiological treatment for Chagas disease was initiated with▁benznidazole.
Regarding AF, heart rate control was initiated with beta-blockers, anticoagulation and transesophageal echocardiography was scheduled for subsequent cardioversion.
After approximately two weeks of etiological treatment for CD, there was reversal to sinus rhythm, without the need for cardioversion of atrial fibrillation.
