A 74-year-old male with hypertension, diabetes and a history of ischaemic stroke, polymyalgia rheumatica and SAHS, presented with left temporal oppressive headache.
Emergency brain CT showed left sinus occupation.
A subsequent MRI study was performed, with findings consistent with mucocele with protrusion and bulging towards the tapeworm.
He underwent CENS by Neurosurgery and ORL, with sample collection for culture, diagnosed with S. commune.
After 24 h, treatment with amoxicillin/clavulanic acid was started at 875/125 mg for 7 days.
In the follow-up examinations, the patient was clinically symptomatic of intermittent headaches. MRI showed recurrence of mucocele with sinus occupation, left sinus congestion, bone involvement and osteomyelitis at the base of the skull.
The PET-CT study was completed with no uptake observed.
The patient was referred to the Infectious Diseases Department where voriconazole 200 mg was discontinued every 12 hours for 6 weeks until the acute phase reactants returned to normal and the patient's clinical symptoms disappeared.
DIAGNOSTICO Chronic sinusitis being the causal agent of the disease the basidiomycete schizum commune.
