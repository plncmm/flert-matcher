A 29-year-old man with a history of multiple attempts at self-lysis came to the emergency department for dizziness, blurred vision, liquid diarrhea, nausea, sweating, and tremors after 48 hours of Negerrium infusion.
Physical examination revealed a good general condition,‚ñÅmalaise, blood pressure 130/70 mmHg and heart rate 130 bpm.
Cardiac auscultation revealed sinus tachycardia.
The electrocardiogram showed supraventricular tachycardia with negative T waves in the anterolateral face.
Blood count, platelet count, coagulation and blood biochemistry were normal.
Digoxin plasma levels were performed obtaining a result of 0.67 ng/mL.
The patient was admitted and upon request of the pharmacy service, two more digoxin levels were administered at 24 and 48 hours of admission, the results of which were 0.6 and 0.55 ng/mL admitted into hospital.
For these concentrations, an elimination half-life of 138.2 h was calculated, and a theoretical maximum plasma concentration of 0.85 ng/ml after ingestion of adelfa infusion.
At discharge, four days after admission, the patient had a two-day history of soft daily deposition and normal cardiac rhythm.
