A 25-year-old man, otherwise healthy, came with a day of evolution of headache, left paresthesia and ipsilateral paresis that, in 12 hours, suffered disorientation and restlessness.
His axillary temperature was 38.2 °C.
Brain CT and MRI were normal.
CSF showed▁lymphocytic pleocytosis and proteinorrhachia.
Treatment was initiated with acivlovir, ampicillin and ceftriaxone i.v., which was interconnected when PCR and CSF cultures ruled out bacterial or viral etiologies.
PCR for SARS-CoV-2 was negative in CSF but positive in nasopharyngeal swabs.
On day 2, the patient became completely asymptomatic except for amnesia of the previous two days.
