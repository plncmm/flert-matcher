A 13-year-old patient, allergic to grass pollen, was referred to our clinic for presenting right nasal congestion without rhinorrhea with right maxillary pain and right malar erythema of two months of evolution refractory to treatment with antibiotics.
Afebril.
Nasofibroscopy showed edema of the nasal mucosa and inferior and middle turbinate without other signs.
It provides radiographs of the sinuses with partial opacification of the right maxillary sinus.
Oral and topical nasal corticosteroid treatment was prescribed.
Performed clinical placement one month later, CT scan of the breasts was requested.
While waiting for its realization, the patient provides a specimen of worm that reports having left his right nostril the night before.
Microbiological analysis confirms Enterobius vermicularis infection.
A single dose of 400 mg of Albendazole was prescribed, with progressive clinical improvement during the following months.
No more specimens were observed in the nasal cavity during subsequent examinations.
