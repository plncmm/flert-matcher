A 24-year-old male, with no relevant past medical history, presented to the emergency department with a 12-hour history of fever and a single painful scrotum lesion.
The patient reported fever of up to 38oC of a week of evolution labeled pharyngitis, and treated with amoxicillin clavulanic acid.
There is no previous history of trauma or exposure to animals, sexually transmitted diseases or tuberculosis.
On physical examination, a flat erythematous lesion was observed, circumscribed with necrotic edge in the left hemicrotum, and painful to pitting.
After 24 hours of evolution there is evidence of increased diameter of the lesion presenting necrotic center and erythematous edges.
Both testicles are normal on examination.
No inguinal lymphadenopathy was observed in the nalytic way, leukocytosis (14000/ml) and increased glomerular sedimentation rate were observed.
Urocultive and urinary sediment were normal.
Testicular ultrasound ruled out testicular involvement, except for testicular covered edema.
Chest and abdominal X-rays showed no abnormalities.
