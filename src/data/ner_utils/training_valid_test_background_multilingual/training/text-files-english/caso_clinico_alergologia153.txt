A 28-month-old child who attended our clinic in 2012 for presenting erythema and facial edema with occasional vomiting in relation to the intake of legumes, fish, carrots and rice with gambling.
In the initial allergy workup, the patient showed positive skin tests for all fish, all legumes, carrots, mussels and shrimp with specific IgE positive for these foods.
In the medical control at 3 months after diagnosis the patient presents reactions with tomato, soy, pumpkin, green and red peppers and grasses for broth, showing positivity to all these foods in vitro and in vivo tests vegetables.
Skin prick tests positive for LTP, profilin and polcalcin.
The study with ImmunoCAP ISAC112 shows a polished patient against the specie-specific specie and the ancestors with cross-reactivity.
Treatment with omalizumab was started in August 2013.
In May 2014, it was found that the skin tests for vegetables and squid had been negative.
Good oral tolerance was found with raw tomato with skin and carrot in September 2014, with squid in April 2015, with almond in May 2015 and with peas in July 2015.
