A 67-year-old male patient with type II diabetes mellitus.
The patient had a 7-day history of diarrheal evacuations with mucus and blood on the 6th day 8th day, fever, nausea and vomiting of gastric contents, as well as generalized lower quadrant abdominal pain in the last 3 days.
On physical examination she presented tachycardia, tachypnea and hyperthermia of 38.7C.
Abdomen, muscle endurance, acute depression positive sign, and decreased peristalsis.
Laboratory tests with leukocytosis, neutrophilia and bandemia.
In simple abdominal plaques standing and lying down, dyspnoea intestinal loops and pneumatosis of the cecum wall were observed.
Radiological image revealed intestinal assa assa and pneumatosis in the cecum.
Radiological image with more details of pneumatosis in the cecum.
At laparotomy, areas of necrosis were found throughout the colon, with fibrinopurulent creams, and crepitation in the cecum.
Subtotal colectomy was performed.
Indirect hemagglutination tests and PCR supported the diagnosis of invasive amibiasis.
Histopathology revealed▁coccidioidomycosis▁lymphocytic infiltration of the colon wall.
Trophozoites of Entamoeba histolytica in the colon wall identified in the histopathological study of the patient.
