A 46-year-old man from Wuhan (China), otherwise healthy, came to our emergency department with persistent fever of 9 days onset.
The patient had been found well up to 9 days before admission, when he had a fever of 38.3 °C without respiratory symptoms such as rhinorrhea, nasal congestion, sore throat, cough, expectoration or dyspnea.
He had traveled to Japan 3 days before admission and went to our hospital.
On admission, her vital signs were as follows: temperature of 1 °C; blood pressure of 120/82 mmHg; pulse of 80 l.p.m.
; respiratory rate of 18 r.p.m.
; oxygen saturation of 95% in ambient air.
Physical examination showed no crackles.
A rapid antigen test for influenza was negative.
A chest CT scan revealed bilateral ground glass opacities.
The PCR test for coronavirus of severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS-CoV-2) was positive.
