We report a case of a 41-year-old male with HS Hurley III sprout.
Family history: Madre diagnosed with HS Hurley I. Personal history: diagnosed with HS since 16 years old.
Smoker of 1 pack of tobacco per day for more than 20 years
Not diabetic.
Chronic autoinflammatory syndrome SAPHO/PAHS (Pyoderma hypersensitivity/Acnecritis) in 2013.
We have phagocytic syndrome reactive to visceral leishmaniasis in June 2018, by immunosuppression by biological treatment.
Fournier's▁gangrene in November 2018 required debridement of the entire anterior abdominal wall and skin grafts.
Hepatic cholestasis secondary to leishmaniasis.
Cumulative colitis and secondary colitis associated with low-dose corticotherapy for 10 years
Cardiomyopathy secondary to cardiotoxicity in 2019.
Treatments: He has followed different systemic treatments: oral▁steroids, antibiotics, corticosteroids,▁cyclosporine, infliximab, adalimumab; always with little therapeutic response.
Topical treatment with topical ATB, antiseptics, topical tracholimus.
Ev: in the last two years, has had several hospital admissions for new sprouts, reactivation of pyoderma ureteroidal effusions and overinfections by Pseudomona aeruginosa and Staphylococcus aureus resistant to sealants (AU)
She needed to be admitted again in March due to worsening of her lower limb injuries.
