A 9-year-old male, with no family or personal history of interest, who consulted the Pediatric Emergency Department of our center for vesicular lesions on the external face of the right arm in the area of Lawde tatumisaje.
The patient reported tattoo placement 15 days before, with onset of symptoms within 48 hours prior to consultation.
He was treated with amoxicillin-clavulanate from the beginning of the clinic, indicated in outpatients for suspected superinfection.
At the time of evaluation in the emergency department, treatment with Silvederma, oral methylprednisolone and hydroxyurea was prescribed, with progressive improvement in the subsequent week.
The patient was subsequently evaluated in the Pediatric Allergy Clinic, and hypopigmentation was observed in the area of application of the tattoo.
True-test was performed with reading at 48 and 96 hours, with positive response to paraphenylenediamine.
