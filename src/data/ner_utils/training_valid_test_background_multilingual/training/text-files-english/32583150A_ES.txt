A 5-year-old boy with no relevant history presented with fever, cough and abdominal pain of several days duration.
His condition worsened to a cardiogenic shock and was admitted to our institution, where he tested positive for antibodies to COVID-19 and had high levels of IL-6.
She developed pulmonary insufficiency requiring extracorporeal membrane oxygenation (ECMO).
After 5 days of ECMO, right reactive mydriasis was observed.
Heparin was urgently canceled and the cannula was removed. An urgent head CT revealed a right middle cerebral artery infarction, cerebral edema and diffuse contralateral subarachnoid hemorrhage.
After CT, she also had left reactive mydriasis.
Reversal of his paralysis revealed absence of movement and brainstem reflexes.
3 days later, brain death was confirmed after normalization of its metabolites.
