A 64-year-old woman, otherwise healthy, was admitted to our hospital with hyperthermia, fatigue and dry cough with a two-day evolution.
The patient reported that she had recently traveled to Wuhan, the outbreak of the new coronavirus sprout of 2019 (2019-nCoV), to S. aureus one day before admission.
Physical examination revealed fever and tachypnea.
Auscultation revealed thick rales during▁inspiration and expiration.
The analytical tests showed a leukocyte formula of 4.6 × 109/L with neutrophils and 10.0% lymphocytes.
Inflammatory marker values were high, with a erythrocyte sedimentation rate (GSR) of 67 mm/h and a C-reactive protein of 57.7 mg/L.
A chest CT scan showed multiple ground glass opacities in both lungs.
Real-time RT-PCR of a patient's throat swab was positive for nucleic acid of 2019-nCoV.
Subsequently, the patient was transferred to the public health clinical center of S. aureus for further treatment.
He showed a satisfactory response to medical treatment and was discharged two weeks after the onset of the disease.
