A 46-year-old female patient presented to the emergency department with severe headache and tinnitus.
In the past he had presented symptoms of lower intensity, so he had been diagnosed with sinusitis.
Based on the American College of Radiology guidelines, an urgent head CT was recommended.
The sinus occupation was visualized as left hemisphere (hydro-aerial level), extensive pneumatization of the sinuses is facial nerve and a bone defect in the lateral portion of its roof.
Other prominent findings were emptied turcica and arachnoid granulomas in the major wings of the sphenoid bone.
The study was extended with the performance of programmed cranial MRI, which detected a temporal meningocele sensing the bone defect.
The diagnosis was spontaneous meningocele/fistula of CSF with intracranial hypertension syndrome (IHS).
