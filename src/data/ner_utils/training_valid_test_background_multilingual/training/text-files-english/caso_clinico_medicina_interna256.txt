A 65-year-old male with hypertension, diabetes and dyslipidemia.
In a study for constitutional syndrome, adenocarcinoma of the lower rectum is diagnosed and abdominal tomography shows a mass in uncinate pancreatic process, confirming pancreatic adenocarcinoma after▁ERCP.
She received radiotherapy and underwent duodenopancreatectomy.
Cultures were taken from the drains and liquids, with the isolation of Escherichia coli BE, Citrobacter freundii and Klebsiella pneumoniae. Antibiotic therapy was initiated eight days after the procedure piperacillin was administered.
Subsequently, he developed 39Co fever, chills and vomiting.
Systolic ejection murmur was not observed; the patient developed a central venous catheter for postoperative parenteral nutrition.
New hemocultives and drainage cultures are extracted, removing CVV and sending its tip for culture.
Despite modifying antibiotic therapy to meropenem + vancomycin, fever persists.
Subsequently, Candida albicans was isolated in hemocultives, catheter culture and surgical drainage, so fluconazole 400mg/day was added, continuing fever and increasing acute phase reactants.
Echocardiography, fundus examination, Doppler ultrasound of the supraaortic trunks, chest X-ray and abdominal ultrasound were normal.
When pulmonary septic emboli were suspected, a chest tomography was requested, showing septic thromboflebitis due to Candida albicans in▁subclavian vein.
He died in hours due to multiple organ failure.
