A 71-year-old woman presented to the emergency department with abdominal pain.
As personal history obesity, hypertension and non-insulin dependent diabetes.
She presented with abdominal pain located in the right hypochondrium for 72 hours, which began after a heavy ingestion.
It is accompanied by chills and nausea without vomiting.
The physical examination revealed the patient's regular general condition and intense pain on compression of the right hypochondrium.
As complementary tests was performed analytical highlighting leukocytes with neutrophilia.
Abdominal x-ray showed a radiolucent halo surrounding the gallbladder and abdominal ultrasound.
After the findings and consolidation (tender gallbladder, thickened wall (7.3mm), with presence of air in the wall and multiple stones, the decision was made to complete the internal emphysematous cyst with the gallbladder wall and multiple stones.
Abdominal CT highlights the parietal thickening of the gallbladder bubbles and multiple gallstones in the gallbladder wall, together with significant inflammatory changes, diagnosing intra-abdominal contrast with acute gallstones.
The patient underwent emergency surgery by laparoscopic cholecystectomy.
The pathology confirmed a gallstone with combined gallstones measuring 11.3 x 5 cm, diagnosed as colitis and acute urticarial colitis.
Clostridium Welchii was the microorganism isolated.
The patient was discharged after 10 days.
