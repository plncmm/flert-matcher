A 21-year-old woman presented with a progressive growth of a small lesion in the right upper eyelid (RULP) over the past four hours.
She reported having built up in a rural house and had been previously cleaning several farm animals.
She had no personal or family history of interest.
Color examination revealed a 2 mm red insect adhered to the free edge of the PSD.
Artropod attached to the right upper eyelid, with a red appearance.
The visual acuity was of the unit in both eyes (AO) and the rest of the exploration including the background of the eye was normal.
The treatment consisted in the application of a bastoncillo impregnated with mechero gasoline.
The progressive release of the jaws allowed the complete extraction with a forceps.
Eyelid without incarcerated arthropod remnants, once extracted prior local application of gasoline.
Pathological integrity was sent to the Anatomy Service to verify its presence and to the Veterinary Faculty for its identification, being informed as RS or common dog tick.
Artropod extracted and identified as Riphycephalus sanguineus or common dog tick.
The patient underwent examination to rule out the presence of other arthropods and to study possible local or systemic complications.
Serology of tick-borne zoonoses was carried out for six months, which were negative.
