A 17-year-old woman diagnosed with Lennox-Gastaut syndrome (epileptic encephalopathy).
Treatment with valproic acid is continued since childhood and later phenytoin is added by status▁epilepticus and levetiracetam five weeks after its onset enters with hyperthermia of 39oC, pruritic skin rash and facial edema.
Once suspected, phenytoin was discontinued and treatment with antihistamines and parenteral▁steroids was initiated.
And root: generalization of symmetric maculopular exanthema that initially affected the trunk and limbs, intense desquamation especially facial, without mucosal lesions.
Cervical adenopathies, not hepatomegaly.
One month later residual skin lesions persisted.
Leukocytosis, lymphocytosis with atypia
Elevation of CRP, sedimentation rate, gamma transferase, lactic dehydrogenase.
Serology hepatitis, influenza, mononucleosis, herpes: Influenza B IgM positive.
Urinary sediment with bacteriuria
Chest radiography, thyroid and renal function, proteinogram, immunoglobulins, complement study and hemostasis: no abnormalities.
