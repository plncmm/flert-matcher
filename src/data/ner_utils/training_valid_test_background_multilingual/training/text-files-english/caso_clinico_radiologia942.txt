Portable X-ray of the chest frontal projection, mnip reconstruction, which shows diffuse pulmonary nodules, prominence of the pulmonary artery trunk, reflecting pulmonary hypertension.
Incisively, as anatomical variant, cisura of the azygos vein.
Axillary sections of computed tomography showed multiple micronodules centrilobular, bilateral, with diffuse distribution, without apex-vascular gradient, or subpleural perib predominance.
Multiple focalizations to better represent bilateral micronodules centrilobular.
Absence of ground glass areas and signs of fibrosis.
The image shows that the diameter of the segmental arteries is greater than the diameter of the accompanying bronchus, a sign of pulmonary hypertension.
Signs of pulmonary hypertension
The diameter of the pulmonary artery trunk is increased, measuring 41mm.
An increase in the volume of the right ventricle is observed, with bulging of the septum to the left, with trabeculations of the right ventricular wall, which are also signs of overload of the right chambers.
