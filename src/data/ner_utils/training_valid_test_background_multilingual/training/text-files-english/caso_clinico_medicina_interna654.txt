A 25-year-old female patient diagnosed with inflammatory bowel disease and treated with infliximab came to the emergency department due to right costal pain, fever and dyspnea.
It was decided to admit to the hospital to administer ceftriaxone and levofloxacin, considering that it is a severe pneumonia in an immunosuppressed patient.
Three days after admission, and in the presence of fever, a chest X-ray was repeated, with radiological worsening and the appearance of a small right pleural effusion.
No pleural effusion was detected by ultrasound because the pleural effusion was small.
Linezolid was started to cover gram-positive germs, although the patient presented with a poor general condition, no defervescence, respiratory failure and radiological worsening.
For this reason, the patient was admitted to the Intensive Care Unit, where the antibiotic treatment was modified to meropenem and linezolid.
A chest CT was performed to rule out other complications, such as abscesses or necrotizing pneumonia.
