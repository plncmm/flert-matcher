Clinical data: A 2-year-old pre-selective patient who was previously asymptomatic began with sudden headache and vomiting.
She has no diarrhea or fever.
She was treated with augmentine for 6 days due to a seizure disorder.
Cerebral venous thrombosis is suspected.
Description of the findings: Contrast-enhanced computed tomography (CT) of the brain ruled out venous thrombosis. Venous thrombosis was ruled out and left temporal-parietal medial temporal-cortical hypodensity was observed.
Findings suggestive of viral encephalitis.
It is confirmed with positive lumbar puncture for adenovirus.
Diagnosis: Adenovirus encephalitis.
