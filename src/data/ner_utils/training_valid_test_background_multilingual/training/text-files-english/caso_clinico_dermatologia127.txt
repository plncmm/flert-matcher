Anamnesis A 40-year-old male patient, HIV+, diagnosed by infection service due to appearance of lesions in the genital area.
The patient describes an initial single lesion of an evolution in the perineum area treated with betamethasone/gentamicin cream, which did not recur after 10 days and added new oral sores, sore throat.
Treatment with topical and systemic famicides is started when herpes simplex is suspected, but no improvement is achieved.
Physical manifestations: erythematous, scaly plaques in the scrotum and penile body and erythematous papular lesions of approximately 6-10 mm with a erosion center in the bilateral inguinal region.
▁Complementary Syphilis serology, PCR for other STIs and coprocultive, which are negative, are requested.
Histopathological study revealed spongiosis phenomena with neutrophil and lymphocyte exocytosis and apoptotic▁keratinocytes at different levels.
The granulosa layer was absent in some points presenting parakeratosis.
In dermis, there is an inflammatory infiltrate of▁lymphocytic cells, with some plasma cells, which are available around small vessels of the superficial and interstitial capillary plexus with tendency to be available in bands.
Diagnosis inverse pink pityriasis.
Treatment Treatment Treatment was initiated with prednisone 40 mg/day.
Ev sequestration is added to new lesions in the interglottic zone and highlights the presence of a squamous erythematous plaque on the scalp of about 2 cm in the left parietal region.
One month after the onset of oral corticosteroids, lesions began to disappear without new onset, so that progressive reduction of corticosteroids was started until they were discontinued.
