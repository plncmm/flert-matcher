A 45-year-old male, Nigerian, was admitted for weakness and low back pain radiating to IIM in the form of cramps along with bladder involvement by AUR that required catheterization.
The patient had pruritic lesions of 2 months onset in the chest.
No other associated symptoms.
He had been diagnosed with HIV for 20 days in a nearby center without initiating prophylaxis for opportunistic infections.
Parenteral drug addiction and previous transfusions were associated.
The clinical examination revealed hyperpigmented papulonous lesions for symmetrical sclerosis of the▁thorax and neurological deficit in the lower limbs (BM 0/5 on the right anterior tibialis, 0/5 on the right plantar flexion, hypoaesthesia
A MRI revealed an intramedullary lesion with reinforcement of the ring located at T12 level, and brain CT did not show LOES.
HIV was confirmed and CD4 and CV cell counts (CD4 41 and CV 54.653) were performed.
The serological study was extended to rule out opportunistic diseases of the nervous system including CMV, HTLV, ESR, Lupus, Tenia Solium and Epidermoid, with negative results except for IgG for toxoplasma.
CSF tests confirmed a positive PCR for toxoplasma and a CV of 3025 copies/ml of Epstein&#146;s anomaly virus.
▁Cytology and flow cytometry were performed in CSF to rule out lymphoma that was negative,▁concluding as diagnosis intramedullary toxoplasmosis in HIV patient.
Cutaneous lesions of the trunk were biopsied with the results of a perforating foul.
Antitoxoplasma treatment was initiated together with antiretroviral therapy.
After one week of treatment and initiation of BHR, the patient showed significant improvement with partial recovery of motor balance and with removal of probing without incidents.
