A 27-year-old woman with no history, who was a resident of the hospital, presented with odynophagia followed by diffuse arthralgia and an eruption of extended pruritic erythematous plaques, with basically facial and acral involvement.
The diagnosis of urticaria was confirmed by a dermatologist.
No triggers were found except for viral circumstances; routine viral serum tests were negative.
Antihistamine treatment was established.
48 hours later, he reported chills, chest pain and fever of up to 39.2 °C.
Mild▁lymphocytic colitis was present, liver analyses were normal and the C-reactive protein value was 49 mg/l.
The COVID-19 test (PCR SARS-CoV-2) was positive.
She was treated with paracetamol and antihistamines were maintained, with a slow improvement of symptoms.
