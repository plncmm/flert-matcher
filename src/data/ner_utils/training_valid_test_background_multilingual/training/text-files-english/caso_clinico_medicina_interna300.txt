A 42-year-old man presented with pain in the right hypochondrium.
Smoker, drinker, history of myocarditis with normal coronary angiography and echocardiogram.
Positivity for markers of chronic hepatitis B virus (mutual recognition).
The patient presented with a 24-hour history of pain in the right hypochondrium, blistering and increased abdominal▁perimeter, together with edema of the male fetid type.
In analytical control highlights leukocytosis, prothrombin time of 49% and bilirubin 2.59mg/dl.
Abdominal ultrasound with poorly defined area in the right hepatic lobe, atypical and abundant ascitic fluid.
She goes to the plant where she presents an unfavorable evolution, with the appearance of hypotension, apnea, tachycardia, jaundice and ascitic oligate data.
Paracentesis was performed with turbid peritoneal fluid, with 340 leukocytes mononuclear, 1100 leukocytes and 3.7 proteins.
She is admitted to the ICU.
The worsening is progressive, is obtundated, hypothermic, with sustained hypotension and tachycardia.
He had severe metabolic acidosis (pH 6.9, EB – 26), leukocytosis up to 17g/l, hemoglobin 17g/l, INR up to 7.15, bilirubin up to 2.86mg/dl with transcranial 4.5.
Abdominal CT showed left basal condensation, hepatic steatosis with permeable portal vein, stenosis and ascites.
Normal echocardiogram, negative coombs test, smear with leukocytosis by PMN, lactate 27, hemocultive, rose bengala and negative serology.
Ventilatory support, cardiovascular therapy, antibiotic prophylaxis with cefoxime and metronidazole
Finally, he died of fulminant liver failure in a situation of multiorgan failure.
