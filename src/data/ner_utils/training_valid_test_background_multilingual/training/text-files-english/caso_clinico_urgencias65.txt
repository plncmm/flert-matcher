A 64-year-old man presented to the emergency department with polyarthritis in hands and feet for 8 hours.
His personal history included a right ankle fracture that required intervention for years.
She did not report any family history of interest.
He reported having had a cold, sore throat and cough without expectoration the previous week.
He did not report previous trauma or dysthermic sensation.
On physical examination, the patient presented with a right-sided fever, arthritis in the right foot, first metaphaphalangeal joint, and both ankles and tarsals.
He also presented an erythematous dorsal metaphalangeal joint 2nd right hand.
Given the history of catarrhal symptoms in the previous week, she was interpreted as reactive arthritis and was treated with corticosteroids.
Forty-eight hours later, the patient came again due to worsening of general condition, fever (38.7oC) and increased erythema, edema and pain in both hands and feet.
