This is a 46-year-old male patient, unknown patient, with no history of addiction, with a subacute history of 3 months of evolution of constitutional symptoms associated with hypoxia, asthenia and involuntary weight loss 8 kg.
In addition, there was evidence of quantified fever of nocturnal predominance and progressive dyspnea at rest associated with podalic edema.
A transthoracic echocardiogram showed aortic endocarditis with a very mobile aortic vegetation of 12 mm, severe aortic insufficiency, severe mitral regurgitation and congestive heart failure.
A positive blood culture for Streptococcus gallolyticus sensitive to penicillin was documented, so antibiotic coverage was initiated.
In the studies of the office, a computerized axial tomography of the lumbar spine was performed, which showed areas hypodense in the spleen suggestive of infarction.
In addition, a coronary catheterization was performed, documenting a patent 8.3mm aneurysm in the right coronary artery without lesions.
The patient presents criteria for emergency surgery; however, due to the germ associated with this case surgery was performed in search of gastrointestinal neoplasia.
On the eighth day of antibiotic treatment, the patient presented sudden deterioration of consciousness. Computerized axial tomography of the central nervous system showed right intraparenchymatous hemorrhage, so he was taken to the operating room with a purple aneurysm.
The patient died two days after surgery.
