Female 59 years old, born in China.
No relevant medical history.
The patient presented with fever and emesis of 10 days of evolution.
Last trip to China one year ago.
No other epidemiological or exposure data of interest.
Analytical change in liver profile (Bilirubin 2.6 mg/dL and hypertransaminasemia).
Isolation of Klebsiella pneumoniae bacteremia in blood cultures.
A CT scan showed an intrahepatic abscess (4x4x2cm) with thrombosis of the right suprahepatic vein.
Unfavorable clinical signs and symptoms, sepsis and respiratory failure must be ruled out in the intensive care unit.
The appearance of associated pleural empyema and increased abscess (6x6x3cm).
Coverage with ceftriaxone and percutaneous drainage of liver abscess was unsuccessful.
Enterocococo faecium was isolated from microbiological samples, thus increasing antibiotic coverage with Vancomycin.
After surgical drainage, the patient evolved favorably and was discharged with ciprofloxacin.
Currently under follow-up in recovered consultations.
