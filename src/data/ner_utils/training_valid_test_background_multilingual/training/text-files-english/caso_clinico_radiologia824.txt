Clinical data: A 47-year-old man presented to the emergency department of our hospital with swelling and pain in his face, right upper limb and right hemithorax for two weeks, with progressive evolution.
His personal history included mixed liver cirrhosis (HCV + alcohol) and HCC treated with chemoembolizations.
Description of the findings: An urgent CT scan of the chest was performed with intravenous contrast, in which a filling defect was observed at the level of the superior vena cava.
