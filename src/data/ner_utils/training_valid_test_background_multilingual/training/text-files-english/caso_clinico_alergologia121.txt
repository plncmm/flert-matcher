A 12-year-old boy was followed up in the clinic from four for poorly controlled asthma with associated GER.
Prick test and specific IgE negative for pneumoal fixation was positive until 12 years of age for olives and grasses.
Since the age of ten, worsening of asthma and GER, associated dysphagia for solids.
Also, some episode of facial reaction after food intake with traces of milk.
Prick test and specific IgE for casein 6x6mm and 4.80 KU/L respectively.

Milk products were completely removed and Omeprazole and Budesonide swallowed were prescribed, with normalization of endoscopy, and treatment could be withdrawn at 14 months.
Lactic acid deficiency, asymptomatic from a digestive point of view and asthma improvement.
At 12 years of age, urticaria was observed after accidental ingestion of cooked ham (with traces of milk) and at 13 years of age an anaphylactic reaction was observed after accidental ingestion of cow's milk.
