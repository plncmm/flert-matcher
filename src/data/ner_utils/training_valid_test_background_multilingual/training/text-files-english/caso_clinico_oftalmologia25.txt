A 64-year-old woman with no history of interest presented with a decrease in VA in the RE of one week of evolution.
VA was 0.2 in the RE and 0.9 in the LE.
The anterior pole was normal in BE.
Intraocular pressure (IOP) was 20 mmHg and isochoric and normoreactive pupils in BE.
Examination of the right FO revealed papilledema and macular star.
Clinical suspicion of neuroretinitis was based on a series of tests that included blood count, basic biochemistry, ESR, CRP, chest X-ray and cranial CT.
Complete biochemistry, coagulation, rheumatic tests, immunoglobulins, Mantoux intradermal reaction, proteinogram, gammaglobulins, ANA HCV, ENA, ANCA and ECA were also requested HBV, serum tests HBV, HBV, HBV
Funduscopic examination of the right eye revealed papilledema and macular star; the left eye was normal.
Fluorescein angiography showed lesions with «screen effect» with late hyperfluorescence around.
The systemic study of this patient was normal.
Serology by Western blot was positive to bartonella and oral treatment with ciprofloxacin 500 mg every 12 hours for 3 weeks was given.
The evolution was favorable and she experienced a remarkable VA recovery (OD = 0.9).
