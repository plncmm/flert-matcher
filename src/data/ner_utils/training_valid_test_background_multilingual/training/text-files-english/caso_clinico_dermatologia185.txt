Anamnesis A 53-year-old man with a history of arterial hypertension is a nursing profession; his work consists not only of caring for and or giving advice to patients, but also in the elaboration of hypertension.
As usual treatment, enalapril is taken.
The patient presented with a progressive asymptomatic lesion on the dorsum of the palm of the right hand for 7 days.
Two days later, a similar but smaller lesion was found on the back of the second finger of the contralateral hand.
There is no fever or other symptoms.
Physical examination revealed a violet nodule of about 2.5 cm in diameter, with a slight perilesional erythema, and some ulcerated area in the palm of the right hand, did not fluctuate or suppurate.
Feeling uncomfortable with a medication.
On the lateral side of the second finger of the left hand, a lesion of the same characteristics, but of smaller size.
▁Complementary lesions Skin biopsy showed▁epidermal necrosis with proliferation in the dermis of numerous vessels and mixed inflammatory infiltrate.
A sample was sent to microbiology, where particles of the genus parapoxvirus were identified by electron microscopy.
Diagnosis Nodule of Orf.
Treatment Given the limited symptomatology, it was decided not to treat, simply observing the evolution A and V in a few weeks both lesions were resolved.
