Loxoscelism is the clinical picture secondary to the spider bite of the genus Loxosceles laeta, which presents a limited form of hematuria to the skin and another systemic (loxoscelism with visceral fever).
Necrotic cutaneous loxocelism presents livedoid plaque within the first 24 h, a product of vasculitis secondary to bite and inoculation of the poison.
