A 7-year-old female from a native community of Nieva district in the northeastern region of Peru, with 14 days of disease, who began with fever,‚ñÅmalaise, hypoxia.
After 10 days, cough was added expectoration with blood scrapings and respiratory distress, reason why he was referred to our hospital, and admitted to the Emergency Service in poor general condition.
Pulmonary auscultation revealed subcrepitants at the base of the right hemithorax and, in ancillary tests at admission, moderate, microcytic and hypochromic anemia was found without leukocytosis or auscultation.
Chest X-ray and multislice interstitial tomography (MSCT) showed bilateral parahilar pulmonary infiltrate with ground-glass areas and cystic lesions in the left lung base.
Pulmonary radiography and tomography showed diffuse condensation and interstitial infiltrate, with ground glass areas and cystic lesions in the left base, the largest, 5 x 3 cm.
The patient received antibiotic therapy with ceftriaxone and clindamycin, and maintained a stationary evolution.
Both the Mantoux test and the investigation of Koch's bacillus in gastric aspirate and feces were negative.
Parasitological examination of feces showed Paragonimus spp. eggs, as well as Ascaris lumoides, uncinarias and Trichuris trichiura (Franch 3).
For this reason, tricholabendazole was added at a rate of 10 mg/kg in two doses, followed by albendazole at a rate of 400 mg per day for 5 days, with disappearance of both fever and cough.
The control thoracic MET showed a decrease in the bilateral interstitial infiltrate, as well as in the cavitations in the left pulmonary base, and was discharged at 31 days of hospitalization.
