In the second week, Sabouraud agar culture showed growth of black colonies with a cerebellar aspect without air mycelium, while in Mycosel agar no growth was obtained.
A new culture was made to better isolate the colonies, by mold contamination in the lower part of the initial culture.
From the initial culture a direct examination with lactophenol blue was performed in which the hyphae described above were observed.
In the case described here, the patient came to the specialized clinic for presenting a dark spot on the right palm, without showing any other symptoms, as well as apparent predisposing factors.
In the mycological study, H. werneckii was identified as the causal agent of the infection.
