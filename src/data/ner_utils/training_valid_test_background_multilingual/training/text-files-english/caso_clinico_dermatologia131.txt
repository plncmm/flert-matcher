Anamnesis A 59-year-old male, with no known allergies or adverse drug reactions, with a history of hypertension treated with hydrochlorothiazide and enalapril.
He consulted for lesions on the face and trunk of almost 4 months of evolution, neither pruritic nor painful, which do not respond to treatment with topical or systemic corticosteroids.
He did not report fever or other associated symptoms.
He denies taking new drugs, recent trips, or insect bites.
Physical manifestations Multiple erythematous▁papules and erythematous plaques are observed in the frontal region, ill-defined, 1 to 2 cm in size, very painful and non-painful plaques.
In addition, a maculopapular and erythematous rash is observed in the neck, anterior chest and upper back.
No palmoplantar or mucosal involvement was observed, nor were cervical, axillary or visceromegaly adenopathies observed.
▁Complementary lesions A skin biopsy was performed, which showed a dense inflammatory infiltrate in the basal lamina with tendency to epidermotropism, and also perivascular in the superficial and deep dermis.
Immunohistochemistry showed marked positivity for CD20, and lower for CD3.
No light chain restriction was observed.
The stain for treponemas in skin biopsy, serum VDRL and treponemic tests IgG and IgM were positive.
Diagnosis Secondary syphilis pseudolymphomatous.
Treatment Benzathine penicillin 2.4 million i.m. was administered.
A rapid resolution of the cutaneous lesions was observed after antibiotic therapy
