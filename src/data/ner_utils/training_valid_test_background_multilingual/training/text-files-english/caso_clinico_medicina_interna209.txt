An 18-year-old male presented with intermittent fever for a month up to 39oC, accompanied by abdominal discomfort, joint pain and asthenia. The only antecedent was fever of unknown origin, which was treated with antibiotics at 8 years of admission.
On physical examination, the patient presented right and left axillary lymphadenopathy 1-1.5 cm, which was neither painful nor adhered to, without other findings.

- Hypothyroidism.
- Autoimmune: SLE, amyloidosis, sarcoidosis.
