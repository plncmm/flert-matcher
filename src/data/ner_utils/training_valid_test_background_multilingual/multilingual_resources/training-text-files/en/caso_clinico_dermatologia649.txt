Male patient aged 43 years, with no drug allergies or personal or family history of interest. He denies taking any drugs. He consulted the emergency department for intense pruritus of one hour's duration together with the appearance of erythematous, papular, pruritic lesions on the forearms and abdomen after pruning a pita (Agave americana) with a chainsaw.

He told us that he was wearing gloves and that he was completely covered with clothing. He had no other symptoms.

Treatment was prescribed as a single dose of intramuscular corticoid and intramuscular antihistamine (dexchlorpheniramine maleate).

He was discharged with oral antihistamine: loratadine 10 mg 1 tablet per day.
