A 60-year-old man with no history of specific diseases was referred to our hospital because of fever and dyspnoea. He was diagnosed with COVID-19 and hospitalised. On admission, the patient's vital signs were: blood pressure 110/70, pulse = 105 bpm, temperature = 38.7 °C and oxygen saturation (on room air) = 82%, which increased to > 94% with oxygen mask. On examination, crackles were noted in the left lung. He was treated with oseltamivir, hydroxychloroquine, lopinavir/ritonavir and azithromycin. The patient had no specific problems and vital signs were normal, with no evidence of electrolyte disturbances. The troponin I test was negative. Initial ECG was normal. On day 2 of hospitalisation, he suffered cardiac arrest and died after unsuccessful resuscitation.
