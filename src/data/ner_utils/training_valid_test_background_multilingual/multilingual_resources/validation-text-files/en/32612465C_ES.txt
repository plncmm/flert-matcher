A 71-year-old man with a history of hypertension, chronic kidney disease (stage 3) and sarcoidosis (inactive, untreated) attended the emergency department on 15 March. His symptoms were cough, fever of 39.2°C and dyspnoea. She had tachycardia and hypoxaemia, with oxygen saturation of 70% on room air. A flu test was negative. A chest X-ray showed bilateral alveolar opacities. C-reactive protein was 90.6 mg/dL. A nasopharyngeal swab sent for SARS-CoV-2 testing by PCR was positive. He was transferred directly to the ICU, where he was intubated and placed on mechanical ventilation. He met the criteria for severe ARDS, with a PaO2/FiO2 ratio of 76. He was treated with a daily prone positioning protocol. Medication consisted of ceftriaxone (1 g twice daily i.v.) and hydroxychloroquine (initial dose 800 mg, then 400 mg daily). The patient's condition deteriorated rapidly and he died on 20 March.
