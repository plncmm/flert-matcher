A 25 year old woman, diabetic, poorly controlled, smoker, who complained of otorrhoea on the left ear for 6 months, resistant to medical treatment, who came to the emergency department with fever, increased volume in the retroauricular area, otodynia and otorrhoea on the left ear. Complementary tests showed leukocytosis, neutrophilia, elevated CRP and elevated glycaemia. Otomicroscopy showed left otorrhoea, hyperemic tympanic membrane. There was an increase in the retroauricular volume that detached the pinna, a fluctuating collection was palpated and painful to the touch. CT scan showed occupation of the CAE, middle ear, mastoid and left inner ear. It also showed disruption of the inferior mastoid cells contacting a collection with peripheral enhancement and thick walls affecting the lateral and posterior musculature of the neck up to the posterior cervical region measuring 7.9x4x11.1 cm. Surgical treatment included myringotomy, cortical mastoidectomy and incision and drainage of Bezold abscess. Empirical antibiotic treatment was piperacillin-tazobactam. The culture revealed penicillin-sensitive streptococcus intermedius and was de-escalated to ceftriaxone. The patient's evolution was satisfactory and she was discharged with outpatient follow-up.
