A patient was notified for isolation of Neisseria meningitidis serotype C in blood cultures.
Medical history was reviewed: the patient, a 26-year-old woman, had been discharged from the emergency department two days earlier and was at home. She presented with fever, rash consisting of erythematous-violaceous maculo-papules on the hands and feet, and polyarthralgias with no other findings.
She was alerted and came to the emergency department with persistent non-thermometric fever (39oC on arrival), intense pain and functional impotence with clear signs of arthritis in the ankles, elbows and bilateral metacarpophalangeal joints, erythematous plaque with a temperature increase of more than 40 cm on the right lower limb, and trajectory of lymphangitis on the medial aspect of the left thigh, bilateral metatarsal tenosynovitis, oedema of the right eye with inability to open, pain and conjunctival hyperemia. No neurological focality.
Blood tests were requested, showing elevated RFA with procalcitonin of 4.77 Î¼g/dL, mild dissociated cholestasis and spontaneous INR of 1.45. Urinalysis, chest X-ray and electrocardiogram were unaltered. New blood cultures were taken x 3 and a lumbar puncture was performed, which was sterile (Gram negative, PCR N. meningitidis and culture negative).
Ophthalmology confirmed the presence of right endophthalmitis of probable endogenous origin and started intravitreal injections with Vancomycin and Ceftazidime. Admission with isolation drops, anti-inflammatory treatment and intravenous Ceftriaxone was decided.
