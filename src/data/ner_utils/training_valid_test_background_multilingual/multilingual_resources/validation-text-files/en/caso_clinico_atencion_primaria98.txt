Reason for consultation
Febrile syndrome and cervical lymphadenopathy.
Individual approach (anamnesis, examination, complementary tests).
The patient was a 26-year-old male, with no allergic reactions to medication. Smoker of 10 cigarettes/day. Occasional Cannabis user, although he denies being a UDVP. Tattoos and piercings carried out in a controlled environment. In the interview, he stated that he engaged in risky sexual behaviour (MSM). His only personal history was asthma and rhinoconjunctivitis.
He came to the clinic with a fever of 38.5oC, not predominantly of hourly origin, of one month's duration, reported night sweats, and on further investigation reported a weight loss of about 20 kg in the last year, accompanied by asthenia and anorexia.
The physical examination did not reveal any particular findings, and the cardiorespiratory auscultation was normal, rhythmic and without murmurs, with good vesicular murmur. There was a painful, soft, non-adherent left submandibular lymphadenopathy, with no supraclavicular, axillary or inguinal lymphadenopathy. The rest of the examination revealed no other findings.

As for the complementary tests carried out, from the consultation, urgent blood tests were requested, the only thing of note was the haemogram: leucocytes 3,740, platelets 117,000, Hb 11.7. CRP 11.4. The rest was normal. An urgent chest X-ray was also performed with a normal cardiothoracic index, free costophrenic sinuses, normal hilarity, increased density in the left lower lobe.

Family and community approach
The patient is a 26-year-old, single, with average economic and socio-cultural level, nursing assistant, living with his parents.

Clinical judgement (list of problems, differential diagnosis)
Mild pancytopenia. Pneumocystis Jirovecii pneumonia. HIV Category 3.

Action plan and evolution
Admission to internal medicine with a diagnosis of pancytopenia and respiratory infection. During admission, serology was performed and the result was HIV positive, with CD4 count: 21 and viral load: 1,199,615 copies, the rest negative.

Mantoux was also requested and was negative. Bronchoaspirate (BAS): Positive for Pneumocystis Jirovenci in Giemsa. BAAR and Gram: negative. Bacteria and mycobacteria culture: negative. Antibiotic treatment with Sulfamethoxazole/Trimethoprim was performed. After assessment by the nutrition unit, dietary supplements were added. And triple antiretroviral therapy with Emtricitabine/Tenofovir/Bictegravir was started. At discharge the patient was asymptomatic at home.
