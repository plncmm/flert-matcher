75-year-old patient with a history of TB, BPH and bilateral cataract undergoing right hemicolectomy with mesh placement and umbilical hernioplasty who was admitted to the intensive care unit, reintervened for intestinal occlusion and underwent exploratory laparotomy; dressings were performed with hydrocolloid dressing 5 to 6 times a day, so it was decided to institute TPN for drainage of the infected mesh.
 Results: The patient's evolution was satisfactory, with resolution of the seroma in one week, which allowed dressings to be performed every 48 hours, with a better quality of life for the patient. And the cure of the infected mesh was transferred to the ward with continuity of cures with TPN.
