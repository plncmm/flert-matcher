Anamnesis A 52-year-old man with a history of paranoid schizophrenia in psychiatric follow-up, psoriasis under follow-up, received no active treatment at present.
The patient is independent for basic activities of daily living, suffers from mental illness and lives with his father.
In September 2016, a lung adenocarcinoma T2 N1 M1 (stage IV) was diagnosed due to pleural involvement and carcinomatous lymphangitis, EGFR, ALK and PD-L1 negative secondary pleural effusion due to left pleural effusion.
24-09-16 starts the first line treatment for metastatic disease according to cisplatin-pemetrexed scheme, receiving a total of 6 cycles, the last one on 07-01-17, with excellent tolerance CT.
The 28-01-17 group started maintenance treatment with pemetrexed.
After the fourth cycle of maintenance, the patient remains asymptomatic and in partial response.
It receives the 6th cycle on May 12, 2015.
On May 19, 17 the patient goes to the doctor's office in a non-programmed way because he says that for 3-4 days there have begun a few spots on his chest that appear very pruriginously
On the other hand, he says he is well, has no constitutional syndrome or any other symptoms.
Physical features: Three clusters of seborrheic keratosis on the intermamarian line and on the shoulders stand out.
Each of these groups consists of about 30 lesions between 0.5 and 2 cm in diameter.
It also presents scratching lesions in these areas.
The rest of the physical examination is anodyne.
▁Complementary tests We ask for a complete analytical result that is normal, and a CT scan of the chest, abdomen and pelvis showing progression of the primary tumor and several adenopathies at the subaortic level 41 cm, the largest axis.
Diagnosis We present a paraneoplastic cutaneous syndrome known as Leser-Trélat syndrome in a 52-year-old man with progressive stage IV lung adenocarcinoma.
Treatment Because it is a paraneoplastic syndrome, the treatment is that of the neoplasm, with which 23-05-17 we initiated a second line of non-metastatic disease with docetaxel alone or in combination with large vessels.
