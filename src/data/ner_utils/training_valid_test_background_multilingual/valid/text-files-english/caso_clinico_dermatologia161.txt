Anamnesis A 61-year-old male patient, born in Peru, with no known drug allergies.
As medical and surgical background stand out HIV stage A2 in treatment with Eviplera, a latent tuberculosis infection treated with isoniazid, syphilis in 2001 treated in Peru, a hyperlipidemia with cutaneous steatosis and one liver steatosis.
He came to the emergency room of our center for skin lesions of one week of evolution, slightly pruritic distributed by thoracic region and later upper and lower limbs and penis.
She had a history of pharyngitis two weeks earlier, so she took amoxicillin and ibuprofen and diarrhea one week before she was treated with loperamide.
The patient denied fever or▁malaise.
One month before she had stopped antiretroviral treatment for one week.
Physical condition Patient with good general condition, affliction.
It presents small circular or oval erythematous plaques of darker center and clearer periphery in both regions inguinal, axillary, shoulders, base of the neck, lumbar region and isolated foot on the back of the right hand.
Some plaques are eroded.
In the middle region of the balanoprepucial groove and base of the glans, an erythematous plaque was observed.
In the oral cavity, the patient presented erythema of the pharyngeal▁pillars and a small erosion in the left pharyngeal pillar.
In the left labial commissure presents a small erosion with erythematous-violaceous base.
▁Complementary laboratory blood tests showed complete blood count and biochemistry, with no significant alterations.
Serology for syphilis and IgM for Mycoplasma were negative.
A 4mm punch biopsy of an inguinal region lesion showed an epidermis with orthotic and parakeratotic▁keratinization, necrotic▁keratinocytes in the basal layer and foci of degeneration.
In the superficial dermis, there was a perivascular and liquenoid inflammatory infiltrate with▁lymphocytic predominance with some▁eosinophils and areas of▁epidermal exocytosis, in addition to colloid bodies and melans.
The middle dermis shows perivascular inflammatory infiltrate with lymphocyte predominance, with some eosinophil granulocytes.
The deep dermis showed no significant histological alterations.
Conclusion: Histology compatible with fixed diagnosis Histology With data from the anamnesis and physical examination, as well as compatible biopsy, establishes the diagnostic suspicion of fixed rash Treatment It is recommended to avoid the possible triggers of cutaneous rashes with 24 hours.
Sepsis was performed at 1 and a half month after the first visit, with the objective of achieving a resolution of the lesions with residual post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation.
The patient was referred to allergy clinics for determination of the drug that caused the condition.
The patch test is performed with the potentially harmful drugs (amoxicillin, ibuprofen and loperamide), being negative for the three drugs.
However, given the low sensitivity of this test with some drugs, its negativity is not indicative of misdiagnosis.
