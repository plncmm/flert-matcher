A 75-year-old man with no comorbidities presented to the emergency department with abdominal pain and vomiting for two days, together with worsening of cough and dyspnea for two weeks.
On admission, oxygen saturation was 88% in room air, but the rest of its manifestations were within normal limits.
The electrocardiogram showed sinus rhythm with no ischemic changes.
Laboratory tests showed leukocytosis (18.1 x 109/L),‚ñÅlymphocytic leukemia (0.9 x 109/L) and a mild thrombocytosis (497 x 109/L).
The value of the Day Day Day was high (3.2 mg/L), but troponin T and renal and hepatic functional indicators were normal.
The chest X-ray showed the possibility of infection with SARS-CoV-2.
An intravascular thrombus was observed in the descending aorta with embolic occlusion of the superior mesenteric artery TAC in an angiography, but without signs of descending coronary artery occlusion.
Catheter thrombolysis was initiated, but the patient showed worsening of abdominal symptoms and underwent laparotomy, with resection of 150 cm of ischemic small intestine.
