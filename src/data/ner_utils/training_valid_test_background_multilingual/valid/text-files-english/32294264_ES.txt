A 28-year-old woman with no medical history reported dry cough, nasal congestion, fatigue, myalgia and arthralgia, without fever.
The coronavirus tests were positive.
As she was in good condition, home confinement was recommended.
Four days later she reported diarrhea, ageism and anosmia.
During the following days she began to feel better, but with persistent dry cough, ageism and anosmia.
The first four days she only took paracetamol without other drugs.
▁Thirteen days after the last dose of paracetamol (10 days after the last dose), the patient noticed pruritic lesions in both of them. At the same time, she observed erythematous▁papules both cutaneous lesions, with no other evidence.
He stated that he did not wear tight stockings or▁shoes or any other type of local pressure that could explain the distribution of the lesions.
Local corticosteroid treatment was recommended.
Despite treatment, three days later, the lesions persisted and passed to erythematous, hardened and pruritic plaques.
At this time, the differential diagnosis was considered urticaria, urticarial vasculitis, idiopathic plantar hidradenitis and neutrophilic dermatosis.
However, no biopsy was performed.
