On arrival to the plant, the patient was hemodynamically stable, with adequate heart rate and fever.
Moderate cachexia.
Aware and oriented, bradyskic.
Mucocutaneous Pallor.
No changes in ENT area.
Cardiorespiratory arrest and abdominal exploration revealed no relevant findings.
No de novo skin lesions.
Neurological examination showed no motor deficit or gross motor abnormalities, normal cranial nerves.
No cerebellar alterations.
Osteotendinous present and symmetrical features.
As regards complementary tests, the emergency laboratory tests showed a slight elevation of bilirubin to direct elimination (total 1, 37 mg/dl, direct 0,86 mg/dl) and hemoglobin 75 g/l of macrocytic characteristics.
A CT scan of the skull did not describe EON, hemorrhagic or ischemic foci.
A lumbar puncture was performed and showed no pathological findings.
Analytical profiles called attention to severe vitamin B12 deficiency (12, 50 pg/ml) with normal thyroid hormones.
Serology for intermediate negative fever (including syphilis, HIV hepatitis).
In addition, ESR, EBV, B. burdgoferi were negative.

A CT scan of the chest, abdomen and pelvis was performed, describing a prostatic hyperplasia and a thickening of the circumferential 10 mm in the rectum. The pathological findings were requested but not visual.
The electroneurogram showed a predominantly sensory and axonal polyneuropathy.
The diagnosis of anemia due to vitamin B deficiency was reached
The etiology of this deficit was sought, so the anti-parietal antibodies, intrinsic anti-factor and celiac study (antibodies and HLA) were requested, which was negative.
Upper endoscopy showed chronic atrophic gastritis with metaplasia and duodenum without alterations.
H. Pylori negative.
Finally, a diagnosis of anemia due to vitamin B12 deficiency secondary to chronic atrophic gastritis with metaplasia was established.
