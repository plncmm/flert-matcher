A 64-year-old male, alcoholic.
She came to the emergency room with abdominal pain of 5 days duration, vomiting and fever.
Pallor, sweating, T° 38°, HR 102 bpm, normotensive.
The consolidation of intense epigastric pain with voluntary defense.
Leukocytosis with neutrophilia.
The ECG, abdominal and chest X-ray showed no abnormalities.
Analgesia was administered without improvement.
Abdominal CT with contrast IV: distended stomach, marked diffuse thickening of the gastric walls with intramural hypodensity and peripheral enhancement.
Hyperdensity of perigastric fat.
Absence of pneumoperitoneum.
With these findings and a history of alcoholism phlegmonous gastritis is suspected, establishing intravenous antibiotic treatment.
During admission, gastroscopy revealed thickened, edematous and erythematous gastric folds .
The biopsies obtained showed acute submucosal inflammation and focal microabscesses.
Mixed flora was cultured with predominance of Streptococcus faecalis.
