A 25-year-old woman, with uncontrolled diabetes, smoker, referred to left otorrhea of 6 months of evolution resistant to medical treatment, who came to the emergency department with fever, increased volume of the retroauricular area or left otodynia.
▁Complementary tests showed leukocytosis, neutrophilia, elevated CRP and high blood glucose.
Otomicroscopy revealed left otorrhea, hyperemic▁tympanic membrane.
Retroauricular volume increase that clears the pinna, is painful collection fluctuating to the touch.
CT showed occupation of EAC, middle ear, mastoid and left inner ear.
It also showed disruption of the inferior mastoid cells in contact with a collection with peripheral enhancement and thick walls affecting the lateral and posterior muscles of the neck to the posterior cervical region of 7.9x4 cm.
Surgical treatment included myringotomy, cortical mastoidectomy and incision and drainage of Bezold abscesses.
Empirical antibiotic treatment was piperacillin-tazodone.
The culture revealed a penicillin-sensitive Streptococcus intermedius, and ceftriaxone was ruled out.
The evolution of the patient was satisfactory, she was discharged with outpatient follow-up.
