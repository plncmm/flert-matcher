A 28-year-old man, born in Argentina, without known drug allergies, occasional cannabis smoker.
She had no relevant history or regular medication.
fever of up to 39.5oC, of four weeks of evolution, predominantly at night, associated with odynophagia, cough, rinitis and headache, as well as a loss of 7 kg at night
Epigastric pain and pasty stools were present in the last two weeks, with vomiting appearing 48 hours before the consultation.
He was admitted with normal blood pressure, eupneic and fever.
The physical examination revealed cachexia and cutaneous dryness.
She had painful lymphadenopathies of less than 1 cm laterocervical, supraclavicular and bilateral inguinal.
Abdominal palpation revealed epigastric pain, without peritoneal irritation, and hepatosplenic enlargement, with no other relevant findings.
Laboratory tests showed elevated C-reactive protein of 9 mg/dL, increased glomerular sedimentation rate (60 mm/h) and ferritin (1477 ng/ml).
Leukocytosis of 34,000/mm3 and hypereosinophilia of 23,000/mm3 stood out.
The patient had abnormal liver profile with cytolysis (ALT/AST 90/40 U/L) and stasis (GGT/FA 304/624 U/L).
Anti-infectious mononucleosis antibodies (Virus Epstein-style), serology for HIV and hepatotropic viruses were negative.
The chest X-ray showed parahilar thickening, with no other relevant findings.
The study continued with abdominal echography in which hepatosplenic enlargement and retroperitoneal lymphadenopathies of pathological aspect stood out, so toco-abdominal CT was performed, presenting involvement of the retroperitoneal mediastinal pulmonary ligament and hepatopathic pulmonary hylioma.
Cryptococcus neoformans grubii variety was isolated from the patients with hemocultive lesions on the day of admission.
A lumbar puncture was performed with the presence of Cryptococcus antigen.
Due to the presence of mediastinal adenopathies, a fiberoptic▁bronchoscopy with bronchoalveolar lavage and EBUS was performed with subcarinal lymph node biopsy, which showed fungal structures of the Cryptococcus type.
With the diagnosis of disseminated cryptosis in immunocompetent patient (primary and secondary immunodeficiencies, autoimmune disorders and▁neoplasms were ruled out), treatment with amphotericin B and flucytosine was performed for two weeks.
Subsequently, she was switched to oral fluconazole until completing one year of treatment, with good clinical evolution and progressive decrease in eosinophil count.
