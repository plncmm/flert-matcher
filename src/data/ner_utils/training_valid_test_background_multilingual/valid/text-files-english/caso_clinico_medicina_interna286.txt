He had chronic scratching lesions on trunk and abdomen.
Analytical analysis showed total bilirubin 5"18 mg/dl (direct 2"87 mg/dl), GOT 444 mg/dl, GGT 183 mg/dl and alkaline rest 239 mg/dl.
Abdominal ultrasound was also requested, which showed the presence of a large trabeculated cyst in the left hepatic lobe (10x11 cm), compatible first with hydatid cyst.
Serology of hepatotropic virus and HIV were performed during admission, all of them negative.
CT, abdominal MRI and cholangio-MRI were performed, showing a large multi-stage cystic lesion in hepatic segment IV with intrahepatic biliary dilatation and secondary cystic fibrosis due to mass compression.
Finally, serology of Echinococcus granulosus was requested, which was positive (IgG), giving the key to the diagnosis.
She was treated with Albendazole 400mg/12h and currently the patient is waiting for surgery for excision of the cyst.
