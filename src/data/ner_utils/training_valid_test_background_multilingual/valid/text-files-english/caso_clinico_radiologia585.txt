We report the case of a 26-month-old girl who came to the emergency department with a 4-day history of high fever and progressive abdominal pain.
ECHO: cystic lesion of thick walls and heterogeneous content located in the anterior supravesical region.
Contrast-enhanced CT: a cystic lesion of 55 mm maximum diameter skull-type, located in the urachus path between the obligate and the bladder dome, adjacent to the anterior abdominal wall.
It has thick walls that intensely capture contrast, with intralesional septa, so its appearance is the very superinfected uracal cyst.
Percutaneous drainage of the collection was performed obtaining purulent fluid, which was referred for the practice of microbiological and cytological study.
Microbiology: abundant colonies of Staphylococcus aureus.
