A 59-year-old patient with fever and emetic syndrome of one week duration, pain in the right hypochondrium, Murphy negative.
Leucocytosis, 21,000, PCR, 300.
With the clinical suspicion of febrile syndrome with abdominal focus CT was requested.
Abdominal CT confirmed the presence of a focal hypodense hepatic lesion (condition 15-20 HU), located in the right hepatic lobe, with lobulated contours and septa, with marginal and septal enhancement.
This lesion conditioned obliteration of the medial suprahepatic vein and stenosis of the right superior portal branch.
Discrete amount of free fluid in the pelvis.
The rest of the abdominal structures showed no significant alterations.
