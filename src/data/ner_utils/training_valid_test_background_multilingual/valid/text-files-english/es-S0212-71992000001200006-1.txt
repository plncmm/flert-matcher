A 49-year-old male, Peruvian with 10 years of residence in Spain and last trip to his country in 1995, with no history of interest except sexual aggression in 1996, with severe tearing of the anal canal that caused non-toxic transfusion habits.
She consulted for 4-5 stools a day, liquid, without pathological products, of about 6 months of evolution, adding fever up to 39oC in the last two months.
Pérrez de 2-3 Kg weight at this time; discrete abdominal pain; holocraneal headache.
Anamnesis was performed using a recorder with no recordable data.
On physical examination, the patient had a fever of 38.5°C well, neurological auscultation normal cardio-pulmonary, mild abdomen and diffusely painful rectal examination, with no signs of peritoneal irritation, coughing or constipation.
Laboratory data: Globular sedimentation rate (GSR) 35; blood count, blood biochemistry, basic coagulation study, thyroid hormones and normal urine routine.
Anti Hbc and anti HBe positive; HCV, RPR, Rose of Bengala, Ac.
Leishmania, Ag.
cryptococcal and Mantoux were negative.
CMV antigenemia 70 cells; Ac.
Positive toxoplasma HIV (ELISA and Western Blot).
CD4 96; Viral Load 153,690 copies.
Negative blood cultures (X3) (2 sodas duodenal study); negative urine cultures for cryptosporidium and microsporidia; negative enteropathogens and Clostridium difficile toxin; negative duodenal juice and CMV 2a culture.
Eye fundus showed no evidence of CMV infection.
Imaging tests: chest X-ray, abdominal ultrasound, gastroscopy, and gastrointestinal transit with no notable alterations.
Cranial CT showed an intra-axial mass with predominantly annular enhancement after IV contrast, centered on the anterior arm of the internal capsule and significant left vasogenic differential lymph nodes associated with toxoplasmosis.
Treatment with 48 Ganvir IV was started for the treatment of CMV infection, as well as Sulfadiacin and Pirim Custolic, the treatment with IV corticoids for the brain mass was started, with disappearance of fever and normalization of hours.
Fourteen days after starting the treatment the patient remained asymptomatic; the head CT was repeated, showing a significant decrease in the size of the lesion, which supports the diagnosis of cerebral toxoplasmosis.
