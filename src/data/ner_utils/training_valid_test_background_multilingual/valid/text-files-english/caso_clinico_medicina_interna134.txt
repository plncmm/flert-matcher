Our case is a 73-year-old woman with a history of gallstones.
He came to the emergency room due to aphasia and decreased sensitivity and strength in the four limbs, with 24 hours of evolution.
She had fever of more than 38oC with tiritone for 10 days and weight loss of 12 Kg in the last 2 months.
asthenia, arthralgia, xerostomia and xerophthalmia.
On examination, the constants were normal except a temperature of 37.8oC.
Neurologically, he presented aphasia, comprehending simple orders, effacement of the right nasolabial sulcus and a tendency to look at the left.
He moved the four limbs with claudication of the right limbs.
Blood tests revealed leukocytes 13,330 with 85.4% N, CRP 8.3 mg/dL, ESR 59, LDH 883, normal renal function, coagulation, ECG and chest X-ray.
Tumor markers, proteinogram, immunoglobulins, TSH, ACE, complement, hemocultive and urocultive were requested and were normal.
Autoimmunity was positive with ANAs pattern 1/320 and SS-A(RO) 53.
A cranial CAT scan showed multiple hypodense foci that affected the lenticular nucleus, caudate nucleus and left radiated corona.
The study was completed with MRI, with the diagnosis of ischemic stroke of left MCA and silent infarcts in the territory of right MCA.
The CSF study and the electroencephalogram were normal.
After an episode of dyspnea, echocardiography ruled out endocarditis and CT angiography showing segmental and subsegmental bilateral pulmonary thromboembolism.
Given the hypercoagulable state, constitutional status and fever, a special coagulation study was requested, which was normal, and a CAT scan where the findings suggested vesicular carcinoma with inconvenience of the liver with hiliar adenopathy.
Abdominal ultrasound showed a 6 cm image in hepatic segment VIII, from which samples were obtained for pathological anatomy and culture by percutaneous biopsy.
The final diagnoses were cholangiocarcinoma and hepatobiliary abscess by Clostridium perfringens.
