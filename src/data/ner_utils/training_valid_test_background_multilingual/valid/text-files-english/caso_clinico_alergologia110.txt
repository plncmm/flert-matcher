A 15-year-old patient with no relevant past medical history complaining of generalised urticaria, dysphonia, dyspnoea and dizziness for one hour after taking ibuprofen and two hours after eating tomato-containing macarrhoea.
Subsequently, the patient presented another episode of generalized urticaria, lip angioedema and dyspnea three hours after eating a medication consumed alcohol, during the reaction he was doing physical exercise.
In both episodes the patient had smoked Cannabis sativa.
Allergological study (intraepidermal tests, specific IgE determination and molecular study) was positive for pollen, epithelia and urticarial lesions.
Prickby-prick against Cannabis sativa was also positive (with negative controls).
Tryptase at 60 minutes of episodes 23.9 mcg/L and 10.1 mcg/L, first and second episode respectively, with normal baseline tryptase.
After the reactions, the patient appropriately treated with macarrones, tomato, ibuprofen and Cannabis sativa.
The immunoblot detected a band (<14kDa) against the sentinel node extract.
