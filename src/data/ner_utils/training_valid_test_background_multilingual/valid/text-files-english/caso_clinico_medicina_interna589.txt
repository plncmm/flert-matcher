We report the case of a 53-year-old man who was admitted to Internal Medicine due to a 39o fever and myalgia in the lower limbs of a week of evolution, with no personal or epidemiological history of interest.
He was treated by his Family Doctor with suspected pneumonia levofloxacin community-acquired pneumonia, without improvement.
On physical examination at admission, the patient was hypotensive, although well perfused and hydrated, with no evidence of sepsis or peripheral adenopathies.
Cardiopulmonary auscultation and examination of the lower limbs were normal.
Painful hepatomegaly of 3 cm and nonspecific skin exanthema on trunk, non-pruriginous and evanescent in less than 8 hours were observed.
The laboratory tests revealed leukopenia, with predominance of neutrophils and significant left shift (23% of falls) and thrombopenia with the red series, coagulation and peripheral blood smear normal acute phase, with no increase in liver function tests with collagen reagents.
Proteinogram, urine analysis, chest X-ray and ECG were normal.
Blood cultures, urine culture and sputum were negative.
Serological results ruled out acute infection by Epstein, HIV, Herpes 1 and 2, Varicela Zóster,▁Cytomegalovirus, Toxoplasma, Parvovirus B19, Borella Rifilensis, HCV,
Seroconversion to Leptospira was observed, with titers above 1:1280.
CT scan revealed findings compatible with pneumonia and hepatomegaly with numerous lymphadenopathies in the hepatic hilium.
Upon admission, empirical treatment with the Flexic Signal was started.
At 48 hours, in the presence of fever, worsening of liver function tests and the onset of an interstitial lung pattern, with normal basal O2 saturation, bilateral cyclophosphamide was prescribed.
After 2 weeks, clinical, laboratory and radiological normalization occurred.
This is a case of Leptospirosis in an immunocompetent and previously healthy patient, with hepatic, pulmonary and▁dermatological manifestations.
