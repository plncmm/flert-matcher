Anamnesis A 6-month-old girl with no history of interest came to our consultation due to lesions in the right half of the scalp for 2 days of evolution that progressively increased.
Parents applied povidone-iodine solution without improvement.
She has had a fever for 24 hours and irritation.
Parents were asked to report that the mother had chickenpox at week 34 of pregnancy, serologically confirmed.
Physical examination revealed grouped vesicles on erythematous base in the right hemicrania and right side of the neck, not exceeding the midline, along with locoregional lymph nodes.

Molecular biology (PCR) study of one of the vesicles showed varicella-zoster virus (VZV) infection.
Diagnosis Herpes zoster (HZ) at C2 level transmitted by placenta.
Treatment Treatment Regimen was fixed at 10 mg/kg/8h oral suspension, paracetamol 1.2 ml/6h and topical cures with mupirocin twice daily for 7 days.
The condition resolves in two weeks, going through the stage of the scabs, which disappear without leaving progressive trices.
