We report the case of a 38-year-old woman with no personal or family history of interest and fishing profession since 17 years of age.
She came to the emergency room with generalized pruritus and haboonous lesions that confluent throughout the body, requiring high doses of corticosteroids and antihistamines for its cessation.
A month later, the same symptoms became apparent, accompanied by polyarthralgia and stiffness, especially in the knees and ankles, with good response to indomethacin.
In the anamnesis, the patient reported occasional consumption of little cooked fish, with no history of abdominal pain.
Blood tests were performed to determine blood glucose, renal function, transaminase, bilirubin, CRP, rheumatoid factor and autoimmunity study, all of them with normal values.
The skin test performed against various foods (milk, fish and nuts) was negative, being positive for Anisakis, with specific IgE for Anisakis of 99.2 IU/ml normal value 0.35 ml.
Upper gastrointestinal endoscopy and endoscopy showed no remarkable pathological findings.
It was recommended not to eat any type of fish, despite which the clinical picture reproduces each time you handle fish or even clothes and material used in the fishery, improving spectacularly the exposure.
The patient in the process of temporary incapacity for work finally became permanent total incapacity, without showing any new episode since the cessation of exposure to fish.
Anisakis simplex is a nematode, described as parasitizing fish, being man an intermediate host, showing a worldwide distribution.
In the case presented here, exposure to Anisakis was manifested in a form of this disorder, with ingestion of a severe and extensive skin affection, with habonous lesions and vasculitic lesions, as well as polyarthralgia.
