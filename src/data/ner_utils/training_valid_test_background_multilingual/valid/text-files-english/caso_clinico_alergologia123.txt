A 10-year-old patient with a personal history of well-controlled bronchial asthma and urticaria treated with antihistamines presented two episodes of urticaria and bronchospasm playing tennis in the absence of cofactors in 2014.
An immunoallergic analytical study was carried out finding only an increase of specific IgE to mites, grasses and olives.
Antihistamine dose was increased and self-injector was prescribed.
persistence of symptoms was decided to start Omalizumab 300 mg/4 weeks.
Two years after the start of treatment, the patient performed normal exercise for his age, seizures, and seizures, with the last dose being prescribed in December 2017.
She has not suffered new episodes of anaphylaxis in these years.
