They report the isolation of Neisseria meningitidis serotype C in hemocultives of a patient.
A clinical history is reviewed: the patient, a 26-year-old woman, was discharged from the emergency room two days before and was at home.
He had fever, rash consisting of erythematous-violaceous macules on hands and feet, and polyarthralgia without other findings.
The patient was informed and came to the emergency room with persistent feverish sensation (39oC on arrival), intense pain and functional impotence clear signs of arthritis, bilateral erythematous lymph node swelling and left lower limb tenderness, and tenderness.
No neurological focality.
Blood tests showed an increase in APR, with a procalcitonin of 4.77 Î¼g/dL, spontaneous stasis of mild stenosis and INR of 1.45.
Urine analysis, chest X-ray and electrocardiogram showed no abnormalities.
New hemocultives x 3 were extracted and a sterile lumbar puncture was performed (Emergency negative gamma, PCR N. meningitidis and culture negative).
Ophthalmology confirms the presence of right endophthalmitis of probable endogenous origin and initiate intravitreal injections with Vancomycin and Ceftazidime.
It was decided to isolate droplets and treat them with intravenous ceftriaxone.
