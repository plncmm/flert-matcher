This is a white male patient, 65 years old, on continuous peritoneal dialysis with cycling (DPCC) for 5 years, who comes to the emergency department for diffuse abdominal pain, diarrhea and nausea of 8 hours of evolution turbid peritoneal fluid.
During the 5 years that the patient was on PD, she had presented 5 previous episodes of peritonitis, 3 with negative culture, 1 peritoneal catheter-related infection of the concatheter and the last, 2 months before the current episode, by Streptococcus Stafilus.
Upon arrival, the patient was normotensive, afflicted with peritoneal catheter exit orifice and showed signs of infection.
The peritoneal fluid was cloudy and the count showed 6,080 leukocytes/mm3 (N 80%, L 20%).
Gram stain showed no germs.
Blood analysis revealed 10,600 leukocytes/mm3 (N 87.9%, L 6.4%, M 4.5%) and CRP 40.3mg/l.
After the extraction of samples for peritoneal fluid culture, treatment with vancomycin and intraperitoneal gentamicin was initiated according to the unit protocol, but in the presence of cloudy fluid and intense abdominal pain, the peritoneal catheter was removed and the patient started hemodialysis.

The patient is questioned about his domestic animals, referring that he has two dogs living outside the house, without having had any previous bite or scratch.
Culture of the exit orifice showed growth of Gram-positive bacilli sensitive to vancomycin.
