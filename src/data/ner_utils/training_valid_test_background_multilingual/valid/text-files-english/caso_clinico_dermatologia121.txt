Anamnesis A 29-year-old male, with no history of interest, who came to the emergency department of our center for having noticed for 3 days a painless lesion on the glans, as well as painful perianal wounds.
She had fever, arthralgia, eye problems, or other skin or mucosal lesions.
She reported no history of▁dermatological or sexually transmitted diseases.
As the only remarkable data, she reported that one month before she had had a complete sexual intercourse without a condom with a woman who was not her usual partner.
Physical examination of the glans revealed the presence of a rounded, erythematous, erosive plaque, painless on the face, with well-defined borders and slightly raised on the dorsal surface.
The right anal margin showed millimetric erosions in saccades.
No other mucocutaneous lesions were observed.
▁Complementary cultures A negative surface sample for Treponema choleradum PCR was obtained.
Serology for HIV, syphilis, hepatitis B and hepatitis C, as well as a sample of perianal ulcers for PCR of herpes simplex virus were all negative.
However, one day after the initial visit, the patient developed dysuria and urethral secretion.
A urethral sample showed the presence of leukocytes in Gram, and urine PCR was positive for Chlamyidia trachomatis.
Diagnosis Crycinate balanitis secondary to Chlamyidia trachomatis urethritis.
Treatment Treatment Treatment was initiated with a 1 g bolus injection.
single dose.
The urethral symptoms and perianal lesions were resolved in the days after treatment, while the penile plaque resolved in the following weeks without the need for additional treatments.
